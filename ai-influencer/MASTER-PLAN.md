## 3. The Rules of the Game

These are the non-negotiables. Break any of them and the project dies.

### Rule 1 — Mira is NEVER a reader
Spiritual is different from fitness. An AI pretending to do tarot readings or channel spirits destroys the whole premise the moment it's discovered. Mira is a *client* of spiritual practice, not a provider. She shares her journey — that's it.

### Rule 2 — The account is SEPARATE from Dalena's brand
- Handle: NOT "lavendermoon" anything
- Name: NOT Dalena
- Never directly linked as Dalena's Instagram
- Mira references Dalena only organically: "I booked a reading with this woman in California"
- If Mira gets banned or exposed, Dalena's real business is untouched

### Rule 3 — AI label ON every single post
The video guy's original account (1.5M followers) got banned because he didn't label content as AI. Instagram will shadow-ban or kill AI accounts that don't disclose. Every post must toggle the AI content label ON when uploading.

### Rule 4 — Batch before posting
Don't post as you create. Generate all 10 reels first, THEN start posting 2-3x daily. This prevents:
- Posting inconsistent characters early
- Running out of steam mid-sprint
- The algorithm flagging inconsistent quality

### Rule 5 — Tell Dalena BEFORE any funnel reel goes live
Her brand reputation is tied to this. She needs to know:
- "I'm building an AI content account"
- "It will occasionally send clients your way"
- "Your brand isn't attached to it — I'm protecting you"
- "Are you okay with this?"

### Rule 6 — Monthly billing, not annual
Don't pay yearly upfront for anything until the concept is proven. $47/mo for Higgsfield is cancelable. $348/year isn't.

### Rule 7 — Images first, video second
Video eats ~10x more credits than images. Lock every still image before animating anything. One bad video clip from a weak source image = 10-15 credits wasted.

### Phase 1 — Image Library (CURRENT PHASE — PAUSED)

**Why:** Video costs ~10x more credits than images. Generating video from weak source images burns the $47 plan in 3 days. Lock every still first, then animate only the winners.

### Phase 2 — Video Animation

**Goal:** Turn the 10 stills into 20 short video clips (each reel needs 2 clips: before + after or hook + payoff).

**Method:**
1. Open Higgsfield → Video → Kling 3.0 (or Seedance 1.5 Pro if audio needed)
2. Upload a still image as the start frame
3. Write a simple motion prompt:
   - "She takes a slow breath and looks down"
   - "She slowly turns her head toward the window"
   - "Her hand moves to her chest, holding the card up gently"
   - Keep it MINIMAL — static cameras, small motions
4. Length: 3-5 seconds per clip (not 8-10 — costs more credits and most reels only need short beats)
5. Resolution: 1080p, vertical 9:16
6. Toggle OFF "camera movement" if it suggests multiple angles — we want static

**Critical:** Kling/Seedance work from ONE start frame. Keep the motion simple enough that the face doesn't drift mid-clip. Dramatic motion breaks character consistency.

### Phase 3 — Edit + Assemble

**Goal:** 10 finished Instagram Reels.

**Method:**
1. Open CapCut (free, desktop or mobile)
2. Import the 2 video clips per reel
3. Add viral audio (trending song from Instagram's "Use in Reel" section)
4. Add hook text (first 2-3 seconds) — big, bold, centered
5. Add payoff text (last 2-3 seconds)
6. Cut to the beat — fast cuts feel energetic, slow cuts feel emotional
7. Export 9:16, 1080p, 30fps

**Reel structure (every reel must follow this):**
- Seconds 0-3: **Hook** — stops the scroll. Visual contrast + text overlay.
- Seconds 3-15: **Body** — tells the story. Mira's journey beat.
- Seconds 15-20: **Payoff** — spike the viewer's dopamine. This is where they decide to like/save.

### Phase 4 — Launch

**Goal:** 10 reels posted in 7 days + funnel live + first sale.

**Pre-launch checklist:**
- [ ] All 10 reels edited and ready
- [ ] Instagram account created (handle locked, bio filled, profile picture = Mira's canonical face)
- [ ] Creator or Business account activated (for insights)
- [ ] AI content label understood (toggle ON every post)
- [ ] Stan Store live with $27 journal
- [ ] Link in bio set up (Stan Store URL)
- [ ] Dalena told about the project
- [ ] Many Chat DM automation configured (trigger words: "guide", "link", "info" → auto-DM Stan Store link)

**Posting schedule:**
- 2-3 reels per day at varied times (morning, midday, evening)
- Stagger posts — don't batch-post all 10 at once (looks spammy to the algorithm)
- Post WITH captions stacked with keywords: glow up, awakening, tarot, healing, shadow work, spiritual journey, divine feminine (even though Mira doesn't SAY these, the captions can include them for search)
- AI label toggled ON every single time

**What to track daily:**
- Views per reel
- Followers gained
- Link clicks
- Sales (Stan Store dashboard)
- Any negative feedback (comments calling out AI — that's fine, we're labeled; comments that are angry = bigger problem)

## 8. The Working Prompts

Full prompts live in `higgsfield-prompts.md`. Here's the overview.

### The JSON Structure (5 Fields — Always)

```json
{
  "subject": "...",      // NEVER CHANGE THIS. Locks the face.
  "environment": "...",  // Only field that changes per reel
  "style": "...",        // Keep same for visual consistency
  "mood": "...",         // Changes per reel
  "camera": "..."        // Changes per reel
}
```

### The Canonical Subject Field (NEVER CHANGE)

```
31-year-old Mexican-American woman named Mira. Medium-deep olive skin with warm undertones and visible natural skin texture including light freckles across the nose bridge. Long dark brown wavy hair parted in the middle, slightly undone, falling past her shoulders. Dark brown eyes with a faint amber ring around the iris. Soft heart-shaped face. Subtle beauty mark on the right side of her jawline, one inch below her earlobe. Full lips, slight natural gap between front teeth when mouth is relaxed. Left eyebrow slightly higher than right. Minimal makeup — just a touch of mascara and tinted lip balm. No filter look. No AI smoothness. Wearing a simple oversized cream linen button-up shirt, top two buttons undone, collar slightly wrinkled.
```

Outfit can change per reel (e.g., "grey hoodie" for rock bottom). But facial features + hair + skin never change.

### The Style Field (ALWAYS USE)

```
Editorial portrait photography. Shot on 35mm film, Kodak Portra 400. Natural film grain visible. Muted warm color palette — cream, beige, soft brown, faded terracotta. Slight chromatic softness around the edges. No HDR, no saturation boost, no digital sharpening. The image should feel like it was scanned from a film negative.
```

### Character Consistency Rules

1. **Subject field stays identical every time** — the beauty mark, amber eye ring, eyebrow asymmetry are the anchors
2. **Always upload `mira-face-only.png` as character reference** — click the + button in the prompt box
3. **Keep the style field identical** — switching film stock mid-project breaks visual consistency
4. **Re-roll before re-prompting** — if a batch looks off, hit Generate again (Nano Banana varies its output)

### Cinematic Prompt Failure Lesson

First "rock bottom" prompt failed because it was too theatrical:
- ❌ Wide shot from across a kitchen table
- ❌ Wine glass + crumpled tissues arranged as props
- ❌ Dramatic overhead lightbulb
- Result: Looked like a movie set, not a real photo

Fixed by:
- ✅ Medium close-up, slightly low angle
- ✅ No props — just her and the floor
- ✅ Soft bedside lamp light
- ✅ Bedroom floor instead of kitchen table
- Result: Looked like someone accidentally caught her sitting there

**Rule to remember:** Strip theatrical elements. Get closer. Use honest light. Real photos aren't composed — they're caught.
